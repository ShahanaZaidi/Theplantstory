Easy care hanging palnts:

  1. Devil's Ivy
  2. Spider Plant
  3. String of Banana's
  4. Boston Fern
  5. Heartleaf Philodendron

1. Devil's Ivy

A robust climber or hanging plant that is full of drama and of fables. An evergreen vine that can grow over one metre tall and scrambles by way of aerial roots which stick to surfaces. The leaves are variegated green and yellow and are alternate and heart-shaped. It thrives in bright, indirect sunlight but will also tolerate low light conditions. Because of its hardiness and low maintenance, it's a popular plant for beginner gardeners.

2. Spider Plant

A clumping houseplant with striped foliage in cream and green and white flowers on long stems. This plant produces spider like plantlets at the end of long, arching flower stems. It is a hardy pot specimen for indoors, patios and ferneries. It is not recommended for planting in your garden beds, as it can spread quickly. Keep this plant moist during the warmer months and drier through winter.

3. String Of Banana's

This popular trailing succulent has leaves that are shaped like bananas or fish hooks and if grown in the ground instead of in a hanging garden, 'String of Bananas' can trail along the ground.

4. Boston Fern

An elegant fern with deeply divided, spreading bright-green fronds, decorated with tiny leaves and a fantastic fountain-like display. Ideal for hanging baskets where it can show-off its dropping habit, indoor containers, patios and verandahs. It prefers a protected spot inside with filtered light and a moist, shaded position outdoors. Easy to care for and maintain Boston ferns are more drought tolerant than other ferns.

5. Heartleaf Philodendron

An evergreen vine that can grow over one metre tall and scrambles by way of aerial roots which stick to surfaces. The leaves are green and heart-shaped. It thrives in bright, indirect sunlight but will also tolerate low light conditions. Because of its hardiness and low maintenance, it's a popular plant for beginner gardeners.
